You don't need the App Store yet. You can use the **Progressive Web App (PWA)** method. This allows anyone with the link to install Kira as an "app" on their home screen.

* **The Pitch:** "I built a privacy-first wealth tracker. It runs offline, and your data never leaves your phone."
* **The Instruction:** Send them your Replit URL (`https://kiramoney.replit.app/`) and tell them:
1. Open in **Safari** (iOS) or **Chrome** (Android).
2. Tap the **Share** button (Square with arrow).
3. Scroll down and tap **"Add to Home Screen"**.

### 2. Solving the "Data on Individual Phone" Issue

You mentioned the data is saved locally. **This is actually a feature, not a bug.**

* **The Benefit:** You can market this as **"Zero-Knowledge Finance."** You (Ian) cannot see their data. It is 100% private to their device.
* **The Risk:** If they clear their browser cache or lose their phone, their data is gone.

**The Fix: Build a "Backup" Button (Critical)**
Before you share this widely, ask your AI agent to add a simple **JSON Export/Import** feature.

> **Prompt:** "Add a 'Settings' page with two buttons: 'Backup Data' (downloads a .json file of my data) and 'Restore Data' (allows me to upload a .json file). This is critical since we are using local storage."

### 3. What to do Next (The Roadmap)

#### Phase 1: The "Soft Launch" (This Week)

#### Phase 2: The "Cloud" Upgrade (Next Month)

If you want users to access their data on *both* their laptop and phone, or if you want to eventually charge a subscription, you will need a backend.

* **Solution:** **Supabase** (an open-source Firebase alternative).
* **Why:** It fits your "Builder" stack. It’s basically a spreadsheet (PostgreSQL) in the cloud. You can add "Sign in with Google," and their data will sync across devices.
* **Prompt for later:** "Help me migrate my local storage to Supabase so users can sync data across devices."
